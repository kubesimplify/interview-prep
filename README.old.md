# interview-prep

A curated interview preparation repository for Kubernetes, DevOps, and SRE roles. Covers beginner to experienced levels with real-world scenarios, hands-on questions, system design, troubleshooting, and practical discussions inspired by real interviews.

We welcome contributions from everyone! If you have ideas for new interview questions, topics, or improvements, please use the following process:

### How to Contribute

#### 1. **Share Your Ideas**: Add your idea to our [Ideas & Contributions Google Doc](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1B2wwVwoCUBNquuOhYEr6TZ54pfDTF-JByO2YW_Xaj1M/edit?usp=sharing)

**What is this Google Doc?**
- This is our **brainstorming hub** where community members propose and discuss new interview questions and content
- This document serves as the **single source of truth** for tracking all contribution ideas before they become PRs
- It allows the maintainers and contributors to discuss, refine, and get feedback on ideas before development starts

**Format to follow:**
- Add your entry in the format:
```sh
Your Name: Your Idea
Topic:
Question:
Difficulty Level:
```
- Be clear and specific about what you want to contribute
- Example:
```sh
Afzal Hassan
Topic: Kubernetes
Question: Add Kubernetes RBAC troubleshooting scenarios for intermediate level
Difficulty Level: Intermediate
```

#### 2. **Details & Discussion**: Expand on your idea in the Google Doc

Provide comprehensive details about your contribution:
- **What type of question?** (scenario-based, multiple-choice, hands-on, case study, etc.)
- **Difficulty level** (beginner, intermediate, advanced)
- **Relevant topics** (Kubernetes, DevOps, SRE, Cloud, Networking, etc.)
- **Specific technologies or tools** involved (Docker, Helm, k8s, Terraform, Prometheus, etc.)
- **Expected learning outcome** or what candidates should know

#### 3. **Create an Issue**: Once your idea is discussed and approved

- Open a GitHub issue in this repository describing your contribution
- Link the discussion from the Google Doc in the issue
- This helps track and organize all ongoing contributions
- Allows maintainers to assign contributors and provide guidance

#### 4. **Submit a Pull Request**: Once your contribution is ready

- Create a new file or update existing ones with interview questions/content
- Reference both the GitHub issue AND the Google Doc discussion in your PR description
- Include clear explanations, expected outcomes, and any examples
- Follow the existing content structure and formatting guidelines
- Wait for review and feedback from maintainers

### Repository Structure

This repository serves as the **single source of truth** for DevOps, SRE, and Cloud interview preparation resources. All content progresses from beginner to advanced level.

### Guidelines

- Ensure questions are practical and based on real-world scenarios
- Include multiple perspectives and approaches
- Add troubleshooting scenarios and hands-on exercises
- Keep content updated with latest industry practices
- Reference official documentation and best practices
- Make your questions challenging yet fair for the target difficulty level
- Provide detailed explanations for answers where applicable
